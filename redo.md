# Redo

Words for re-attempting communication — when you want to try again more kindly.

A shared family vocabulary. Anyone can ask for a redo, anytime.

## Phrases

- **"Can I have a redo?"** — the classic; simple, direct, works for everyone

- **"Let me try that again."** — takes ownership without over-explaining

- **"Rewind!"** — playful, breaks tension, great for younger kids

- **"That came out wrong. What I meant was..."** — for when the words didn't match the intention

- **"I didn't like how I said that."** — honest acknowledgment, models self-reflection

- **"Wait, pause. Let me start over."** — buys a moment to collect yourself

- **"That was my grumpy voice. Here's my kind voice..."** — names the problem concretely, good for little ones

- **"I want to say that in a nicer way."** — clear intention, easy for kids to use too

- **"Can we pretend I didn't say that?"** — lighter touch, good for minor slip-ups

- **"Hold on — I need a redo."** — asserts the need without apology
