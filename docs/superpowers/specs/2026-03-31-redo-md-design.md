# redo.md Design Spec

## Overview

Create `redo.md` — a collection of phrases for re-attempting communication more kindly. Part of the "redoresetrepair" repository, which provides helpful words for engaging with others.

## Context

- **Primary audience:** Family with children ages 5, 7, and 9
- **Purpose:** Shared family vocabulary where anyone can request a "redo" when they want to try saying something again more kindly
- **Use case:** In-the-moment reference when a family member (parent or child) has snapped, been too harsh, or said something they regret

## Format

**Phrase-first flat list** — each entry leads with the phrase in bold, followed by brief context. Easy to scan quickly in the heat of the moment.

Example entry:
```markdown
- **"Can I have a redo?"** — the classic; simple, direct, works for everyone
```

## Content Structure

### Header
- Title: "Redo"
- Subtitle explaining the purpose
- Note that this is shared family vocabulary anyone can use

### Phrases (10 entries)
1. "Can I have a redo?" — the classic
2. "Let me try that again." — takes ownership
3. "Rewind!" — playful, breaks tension
4. "That came out wrong. What I meant was..." — intention clarification
5. "I didn't like how I said that." — self-reflection
6. "Wait, pause. Let me start over." — buys time
7. "That was my grumpy voice. Here's my kind voice..." — concrete naming
8. "I want to say that in a nicer way." — clear intention
9. "Can we pretend I didn't say that?" — lighter touch
10. "Hold on — I need a redo." — asserts need
